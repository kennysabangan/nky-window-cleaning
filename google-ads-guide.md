# Google Ads for Local Service Business Sites — Beginner's Guide

## The Big Picture

You're running local service business websites (window cleaning, pressure washing, etc.) and selling leads to local business owners. Google Ads is how you drive traffic to these sites.

**How it works:** Someone in Florence, KY searches "window cleaning near me" → your ad appears at the top → they click → land on your site → call the phone number or fill out the form → you route that lead to a local business owner → you get paid.

---

## Step 1: Google Ads Account Setup

### Create your account
1. Go to [ads.google.com](https://ads.google.com)
2. Click "Start Now"
3. Sign in with a Google account (create a new one dedicated to this business)
4. **Skip the guided setup** — Google will try to force you into Smart Campaigns. Click "Switch to Expert Mode" at the bottom when prompted. You want full control.
5. Choose "Create an account without a campaign" so you can set things up properly

### Account structure (for 10 sites)
```
Google Ads Account (MCC - Manager Account)
├── Campaign 1: NKY Window Cleaning
│   ├── Ad Group: Residential Window Cleaning
│   ├── Ad Group: Commercial Window Cleaning
│   └── Ad Group: Window Cleaning Near Me
├── Campaign 2: [Next Niche Site]
│   └── ...
└── Campaign 3: [Next Niche Site]
    └── ...
```

**Important:** If you're running ads for multiple sites, you'll want a **Google Ads Manager Account (MCC)** — it lets you manage all campaigns from one dashboard. Create one at [ads.google.com/manager-accounts](https://ads.google.com/manager-accounts).

## Step 2: Campaign Setup (Do This for Each Site)

### Campaign type: Search Campaign
- **Why Search?** People are actively searching for the service. They're ready to buy. This is where local service businesses win.
- **Do NOT use:** Display, Video, Performance Max (yet). Start with Search only.

### Campaign settings:

| Setting | Value | Why |
|---------|-------|-----|
| Campaign type | Search | Text ads in Google search results |
| Goal | Leads | You want phone calls and form fills |
| Network | Search Network only | **Uncheck** Display Network — you don't want your ads on random websites |
| Locations | Target specific cities/zip codes | Not "Presence or Interest" — select "Presence: People in or regularly in your targeted locations" |
| Language | English | |
| Budget | $20-30/day per site to start | You can adjust after you see data |
| Bidding | Manual CPC | Start here so you control costs. Switch to "Maximize Conversions" after 30+ conversions |
| Ad schedule | Mon-Sat, 7am-7pm | When people are actually searching for services |

## Step 3: Keywords (What People Search)

### Keyword match types:
- **Exact match** `[window cleaning florence ky]` — shows only for that exact phrase
- **Phrase match** `"window cleaning"` — shows when that phrase appears in the search
- **Broad match** `window cleaning` — shows for related searches (too loose, avoid initially)

**Start with phrase match and exact match only.**

### Keywords for Window Cleaning:
```
Phrase match:
"window cleaning near me"
"window cleaning service"
"window cleaners"
"professional window cleaning"
"residential window cleaning"
"commercial window cleaning"
"window washing service"
"house window cleaning"
"window cleaning company"

Exact match:
[window cleaning florence ky]
[window cleaning covington ky]
[window cleaning northern kentucky]
[window cleaning cincinnati]
[residential window cleaning near me]
[commercial window cleaning near me]
```

## Step 4: Ad Groups (Organize Your Keywords)

Each ad group should have 5-15 tightly related keywords and ads that match them.

**Example structure:**
```
Ad Group 1: Window Cleaning Near Me
  Keywords: "window cleaning near me", "window cleaning service near me", "window cleaners near me"
  Ads: Talk about local service, fast response, nearby

Ad Group 2: Residential Window Cleaning
  Keywords: "residential window cleaning", "house window cleaning", "home window cleaning"
  Ads: Talk about homes, streak-free, inside and out

Ad Group 3: Commercial Window Cleaning
  Keywords: "commercial window cleaning", "storefront window cleaning", "office window cleaning"
  Ads: Talk about businesses, flexible scheduling, professional
```

## Step 5: Writing Ads

### Responsive Search Ads (RSAs)
Google now uses RSAs — you provide multiple headlines and descriptions, and Google mixes/matches them.

**For each ad group, provide:**
- 15 headlines (30 characters each)
- 4 descriptions (90 characters each)

### Headline examples (Window Cleaning):
```
Streak-Free Window Cleaning
Professional Window Cleaners
Free Estimates - Call Today
NKY's #1 Window Cleaning
Same-Week Appointments
Residential & Commercial
Locally Owned & Operated
Licensed & Insured
5-Star Window Cleaning
Book Your Free Quote Now
Satisfaction Guaranteed
Trusted by 500+ Homeowners
Fast & Affordable Service
Call (859) 555-0100
Serving All of NKY
```

### Description examples:
```
Northern Kentucky's most trusted window cleaning service. Streak-free results for homes and businesses. Call today for a free estimate — we usually schedule within a few days.

Professional window cleaning in Florence, Covington, Fort Mitchell & all of NKY. Inside, outside, screens & tracks. Licensed, insured, satisfaction guaranteed.
```

### Ad extensions (use ALL of these):
1. **Call extension** — shows your phone number directly in the ad
2. **Location extension** — shows your business address (if you have a Google Business Profile)
3. **Sitelink extensions** — links to specific pages: "Residential", "Commercial", "About Us", "Reviews"
4. **Callout extensions** — extra text: "Free Estimates", "Licensed & Insured", "Same-Week Service", "Satisfaction Guaranteed"
5. **Structured snippets** — "Services: Residential, Commercial, Screen Cleaning, Track Cleaning"

## Step 6: Conversion Tracking

This is how Google knows which clicks turn into customers. Without this, you're flying blind.

### Set up conversions in Google Ads:
1. Go to Google Ads → Tools & Settings → Conversions
2. Click "+ New Conversion Action"
3. Choose "Website"
4. Set up these conversion actions:

| Conversion Name | Category | Value | Count |
|----------------|----------|-------|-------|
| Phone Call | Phone call lead | $50 (estimated lead value) | One per user |
| Form Submit | Submit lead form | $50 | One per user |
| Quote Request | Submit lead form | $75 | One per user |

5. Google will give you a tag — this goes on your website (handled by GA4 + GTM integration we're building)

### How it connects:
```
User clicks ad → Lands on your site → Calls phone or fills form
→ GA4 fires conversion event → Google Ads records conversion
→ Google optimizes to get you MORE conversions
```

## Step 7: Budget & Bidding Strategy

### Starting budget:
- **$20-30/day per campaign** is a safe starting point
- That's $600-900/month per site
- For window cleaning in NKY, expect $5-15 per click, $30-80 per lead

### Bidding progression:
1. **Week 1-2:** Manual CPC — set max CPC at $10-15 to control costs
2. **Week 3-4:** Gather data, see which keywords convert
3. **Month 2:** Switch to "Maximize Conversions" with a target CPA
4. **Month 3+:** Optimize — pause underperforming keywords, scale winners

### Expected costs (window cleaning, local):
| Metric | Estimate |
|--------|----------|
| Average CPC | $5-12 |
| Conversion rate | 8-15% (local service = high intent) |
| Cost per lead | $30-80 |
| Monthly budget | $600-900 |
| Leads per month | 10-25 |

## Step 8: Ongoing Optimization (Weekly Tasks)

### Every week:
1. **Search Terms Report** — See what people actually searched. Add irrelevant terms as negative keywords.
2. **Check conversion data** — Which keywords are driving calls/forms? Pause the ones that aren't.
3. **Quality Score** — Google rates your keywords 1-10. Aim for 7+. Low scores = higher costs.
4. **Ad performance** — Google shows which headlines/descriptions perform best. Pause the low performers.

### Every month:
1. **Review cost per conversion** by keyword and ad group
2. **A/B test** new ad copy
3. **Adjust bids** — increase for high-converting keywords, decrease for expensive ones
4. **Check competitor ads** — Google's Auction Insights shows who you're competing against

## Step 9: Google Business Profile (Free + Critical)

Before running ads, set up a **Google Business Profile** for each site:
1. Go to [business.google.com](https://business.google.com)
2. Create a profile with the business name, phone, service area
3. Add photos, hours, services
4. This enables **Local Service Ads** and **Location Extensions** in your Google Ads

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Using broad match keywords** — wastes money on irrelevant searches
2. **Not adding negative keywords** — you'll pay for "window cleaning jobs" and "how to clean windows DIY"
3. **Targeting too large an area** — stick to your actual service area
4. **Not tracking conversions** — you can't optimize what you don't measure
5. **Setting and forgetting** — Google Ads needs weekly attention
6. **Ignoring Quality Score** — low scores = paying more per click
7. **Not using ad extensions** — free real estate that improves click-through rate
8. **Running ads 24/7** — local service searches peak during business hours

## Glossary

| Term | Meaning |
|------|---------|
| CPC | Cost Per Click — how much you pay each time someone clicks your ad |
| CTR | Click-Through Rate — % of people who see your ad and click it |
| CPA | Cost Per Acquisition — how much you pay per lead/conversion |
| Quality Score | Google's 1-10 rating of your keyword relevance (higher = cheaper clicks) |
| Impression | Each time your ad is shown to someone |
| Conversion | A desired action — phone call, form submission, quote request |
| Negative Keyword | A word that prevents your ad from showing (e.g., "free", "jobs") |
| RSA | Responsive Search Ad — Google mixes your headlines/descriptions automatically |
| MCC | Manager Account — umbrella account for managing multiple Google Ads accounts |
| GTM | Google Tag Manager — manages tracking codes on your website |
| GA4 | Google Analytics 4 — tracks website visitors and their behavior |
